Original data source:

Chicago Police Department Investigative Stop Reports

Major data cleaning steps included:

(1)

Removing officers who are in units assigned to the entire city. Often these were special task force officers such as citywide detectives.

(2)

Using fuzzy logic to geocode the stop locations. (Original data anonymized addresses by replacing the final two digits with XX.)

The first pass replaced all XXs with 0s and batch processed the geocoding with the tidygeocoder package in R.

I then filtered the failed stop locations with more than 20 associated stops, and found plausible addresses using Google maps.

(3)

Extrapolating aggregated geographic administrative boundaries for individual officers using beat numbers to compare stop location and officer assignment.

(4)

Subsetting to stops between 2017 and 2019 or between 2021 and 2023 because those were the relevant years to analyze changes related to CPD redistricting that took place in 2020.

Quick note: my dataset was too large to commit to github using normal channels. So I removed most variables, and split it into two files. "data" is years 2017-2019. "data2" is years 2021-2023.
